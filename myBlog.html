Top 5 best road trips in India

Well, a traveller or not, who would want to miss out on any trip, especially after being restricted for more than a year? If you are someone who is always keen to witness the scenic journey travelling from one city to another, also meeting less known places and towns in between, then road trips are the best thing you can ever do for your adventurous soul.

So, here are the top 5 best road trips in India that you should definitely consider for taking a break from your everyday repetitive life.

1. Manali to Leh Highway

On this road trip, you will not only witness the mountains on both sides, but you will also witness greenery, which will take over once you pass the Atal Tunnel. It would be more fun to travel via criss-cross land if you take this trip anytime between June to September.

Distance - 473 Km approx.

Time - 2 days

2. Chennai to Pondicherry

South Indian cities have their own beauty. This road trip is pretty unique from any other road trip. The sea on one side and greenery on the other side will never make you wish that the journey ever ends. Keep your cameras ready when riding on this road. The best time to visit Pondicherry is either during the monsoon or winters.

Distance - 165 Km

Time - 3 hours

3. Ahmedabad to Daman

This will be a heart-warming journey from Ahmedabad to this popular union territory. You will enjoy it more if you visit this place in winter. Daman still has the essence of the Portuguese colonial past, which can be quite fascinating for history lovers.

Distance - 364 Km

Time - 6 hours approx.

4. Bangalore to Bandipur

For those who are nemophilists and love seeing wildlife in their natural habitat, a trip to Bandipur is the one they will never forget. This road trip through the forest is full of excitement, as one may spot animals wandering by the road. It would be a great experience if you visit this national park in the monsoon season, which is between July and September or during the summer, between March to May.

Distance - 224 Km

Time - 2 hours 30 mins

5. Jaipur to Jaisalmer

This road trip will give you a ride from one culture of Rajasthan to another. The Pink city to Golden city journey will change the colours as temples and palaces are made of yellow sandstone. November to March is the best time to dive into the desert and wander in the royal palaces of Jaisalmer. All these places are no less than the others and have their own beauty and culture. The diversity of India will leave you awestruck every time you visit another city or another village.

Distance - 557 Km

Time- 10 hours approx.

Digital Marketing landscape for 2021

Coronavirus not only affected the health of people but the economy too, which led many businesses to shift their focus from the offline world to the online one. But as someone said, “Change is the only constant”, therefore every change brings an opportunity which in this case is making the best use of the digital world in reference to the respective business style.

Digital platforms offer us many ways and techniques to grow our business but which tool or technique will perform better is constantly changing. No matter if you are a newbie to this digital realm or you are an experienced user, you need to adapt yourself to the new trends and keep brushing up your services and content by the hit and trial method.

So here are some digital marketing trends in 2021 which as an enterprise you should get your hands into-

1. Increase in the Voice Search by users

According to the digital marketing expert, Neil Patel, the graph of Google analytics will move downwards because of the increased use of Voice Search.

This is what he said, “There actually isn’t much wrong with it. It’s a great tool, especially considering that it’s free. But marketing is changing. New channels are being constantly introduced, such as voice search.” (He’s referring ‘it’ to the Google Analytics)

2. Depersonalisation; difficult to digest but will pave the way!

Buyers have become smart by coming across the same personalized strategies all over the internet, which is writing names in the email which will not work as effectively as it used to. The problem is not in the personalization but is in the same conventional strategies. So to survive in the digital world you need to come up with new innovative ideas.

3. Use of Long Tail Keywords

Now the marketers have dropped the idea of writing lengthy content and are inclined to use long-tail keywords which are less competitive and help the content get ranked on Google. Long-tail keywords will help your website to get organic traffic.

4. Chatbots

This AI technology will continue to be used by marketers because of its 24/7 services to the customers. These chatbots reply instantly to any of the customers’ queries, which increases customer satisfaction, which is why customers prefer chatbots.

5. Video Marketing

Video marketing will be a trend in 2021 because video brings customers closer to the brand and builds their trust in their authenticity which is important for a business to increase its sales. 72% of businesses claim to see an increase in their conversion rate and 70% of customers share a brand’s video.

6. Influencer Marketing

Influencer marketing is a form of marketing wherein investors use popular personalities to endorse their products. Influencer marketing is not just a trend but is highly profitable as well, for it has also shown a rise in the income of the investors. This is due to the reason that people often tend to consider the products more authentic if a popular and trusted person endorses it and shares his or her own experience. The influencers also help in generating a larger audience by using their own social media platforms to promote a product.

7. Customer Segmentation

It will be more beneficial for a business to launch a large number of small marketing campaigns, targeting segregated audiences than to launch a small number of large marketing campaigns for a large general audience. Grouping of the audiences can be done on the basis of location, behaviour, age etc. This helps businesses to cater to the needs of the customers more effectively.

These trends are here to stay until another challenge strikes but your work never ends, does it? As a marketer or a business, you have to be always prepared for the things which might not work for you in the future even if it is working for now. But by keeping eyes on the fluctuations and deviations happening within your business or outside of it, will make you stand tall and flourish for a longer run.
